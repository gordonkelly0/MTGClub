Introduction

Welcome to the Magic: The Gathering Club! We’re a community of players who love everything MTG—from casual kitchen-table games to competitive formats and deck-building deep dives. Whether you’re brand new to the game or a seasoned planeswalker, our club is a place to learn, play, trade ideas, and enjoy Magic in a relaxed, inclusive environment.

Formats

Constructed

Standard

Uses the most recent sets (rotates every year). Great for new players and competitive play with a smaller card pool.

Pioneer

Non-rotating format using sets from Return to Ravnica onward. Slower than Modern, faster than Standard.

Modern

Large non-rotating card pool starting from 8th Edition. Highly competitive with powerful strategies.

Legacy

Uses almost all cards in Magics history, with a short banned list. Very powerful and skill-intensive.

Vintage

Allows nearly every card ever printed. The most powerful format; restricted cards are limited to one copy.

Pauper

Only common cards are legal. Budget-friendly but surprisingly deep and competitive.


Casual & Multiplayer Formats

Commander (EDH)

100-card singleton decks led by a legendary creature. Multiplayer, social, and the most popular casual format.

Brawl

Like Commander but 60 cards, Standard-legal cards, and usually 1v1. Faster and more beginner-friendly.

Oathbreaker

60-card singleton format with a planeswalker commander and a signature spell. Fast and explosive.


Limited Formats

Draft

Players draft cards from booster packs one at a time, then build 40-card decks. Very skill-based.

Sealed

You open a set number of packs and build a 40-card deck from them. Common at prerelease events.

Cube

A custom draft using a curated card pool. Great for experienced players and unique experiences.
